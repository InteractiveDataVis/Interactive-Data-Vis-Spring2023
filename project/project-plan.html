Project Plan

The project prospectus listed a number of methods for exploring the causes of Utah's population growth as measured by the 2020 census. As a student project to be done at the end of a semester, I've limited the scope and approach to fit the related time constraints.

Scope

In order to potentially explain the state's growth as a result of the effects of public safety, proximity to natural recreation, and possible religious community changes would require the requisition of datasets in beyond the census. Knowing my own proclivities to get lost in research, I decided to limit my visualizations to 2022 census data.

Plan

  1. Create a graph to demonstrate the growth of Utah over the period of the census.
  2. Design graphs showing the types of migrants by where they lived before moving to Utah.
    • By categories like 'from abroad', 'from another state', and 'internal growth'.
    • Demonstrate the US regions/subregions from which people are migrating to Utah.
  3. Visualize some comparisons of Utah with other fast growing states, and determine if the general migration patterns match or differ from UT.

Viz arch

I'd love to have each visualization be navigable in sequence within the same web page.

Implementation focus

My focus is on presenting the data visualizations in a polished way, with appropriate transitions/animations, meaningful interactions, intelligent presentation of information. As a consequence, the graphs made be relatively straight forward.

A note on data processing

Outside of a couple small categorical enrichments and some clean up done on Kaggle, I am using data furnished by the 2020 US census survey. I am not manipulating data outside of Javascript. The reason for this is that I'm confortable transforming data via other methods for analytics from previous job experience. Instead, I wanted to process the data using Javascript to become more familiar with some JS data structures and casting between them. My goal is to become more proficient in Javascript.
